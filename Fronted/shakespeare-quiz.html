Literature in English - Senior 6 Course Summary

Introduction to the Course

This literature course is designed to develop students' critical thinking, analytical skills, and language proficiency through the study of various literary texts. The Rwanda Education Board syllabus emphasizes both the appreciation of literature and the improvement of English language skills.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop understanding and appreciation of literary texts
  • Enhance English language skills through literature
  • Analyze themes, characters, and literary techniques
  • Connect literature to real-life situations
  • Develop critical thinking and creative writing skills

Language Learning Activity:

Identify and define 5 new vocabulary words from this page. Use each in a sentence related to literature.

Prescribed Texts and Analysis

1. "The Concubine" by Elechi Amadi

Genre: Novel (African Literature)

Themes:

  • Fate and destiny
  • Love and marriage
  • Traditional African beliefs vs modernity
  • The supernatural in human affairs

Language Focus: The novel provides rich examples of descriptive language, dialogue, and cultural expressions.

2. "The River Between" by Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o

Genre: Novel (Post-colonial Literature)

Themes:

  • Cultural conflict
  • Colonialism and its effects
  • Tradition vs Christianity
  • Education as both liberator and divider

Language Focus: Excellent for studying symbolism, allegory, and narrative perspective.

Comparative Analysis Activity:

Compare how traditional African culture is portrayed in both "The Concubine" and "The River Between". Write 200 words highlighting similarities and differences in the authors' approaches.

Poetry Study

The course includes selected poems from African and English traditions, focusing on:

Key Poets and Poems:

  • "The Negro Speaks of Rivers" by Langston Hughes
  • "Africa" by David Diop
  • "Building the Nation" by Henry Barlow
  • Selected works by William Wordsworth and John Keats

Poetic Techniques Covered:

Technique Definition Example
Imagery Descriptive language that appeals to the senses "I've known rivers ancient as the world" (Hughes)
Metaphor Direct comparison without "like" or "as" "Africa is a giant in chains" (Diop)
Rhyme Scheme Pattern of rhymes at the end of lines ABAB, AABB patterns

Poetry Writing Activity:

Write a 12-line poem about an aspect of Rwandan culture using at least three poetic devices studied.

Drama Study

"The Lion and the Jewel" by Wole Soyinka

Genre: Comedy (African Drama)

Themes:

  • Modernity vs tradition
  • Gender roles in society
  • Youth and age
  • Cultural conflict

Language Focus: Study of dialogue, stage directions, and dramatic irony. Excellent for understanding how playwrights develop characters through speech.

Dramatic Techniques:

  • Soliloquy: Character speaking thoughts aloud alone on stage
  • Aside: Character's remark to audience unheard by other characters
  • Dramatic irony: Audience knows something characters don't
  • Comic relief: Humorous scenes in serious drama

Drama Activity:

In groups, perform a key scene from "The Lion and the Jewel". Pay attention to how the language reveals character traits and advances the plot.

Literary Analysis and English Language Skills

Key Literary Concepts:

  • Plot structure: Exposition, rising action, climax, falling action, resolution
  • Characterization: Direct and indirect methods of revealing characters
  • Setting: How time and place affect the story
  • Point of view: First-person, third-person limited, omniscient
  • Symbolism: Objects representing deeper meanings

Connecting Literature to Language Learning:

The study of literature enhances English skills through:

  1. Vocabulary expansion from contextual learning
  2. Grammar patterns in authentic texts
  3. Writing skills through analytical essays
  4. Speaking skills through discussions and presentations
  5. Listening skills through dramatic readings

Final Project:

Choose one text studied and write a 500-word essay analyzing how the author uses literary devices to convey their main themes. Include:

  • Thesis statement
  • Textual evidence with page numbers
  • Analysis of language use
  • Conclusion connecting to broader issues

This summary is based on the Rwanda Education Board's Literature in English syllabus for Senior 6 and accompanying textbook resources.
